# Top 100 Highest-Damage Weapons in Elden Ring (Base + DLC)

**The Giant-Crusher reigns as the undisputed highest pure-physical damage weapon in Elden Ring, reaching ~965 AR when two-handed at 80 STR with Heavy affinity — but raw Attack Rating alone does not tell the full story.** Weapons like the Blasphemous Blade (universally ranked #1 for PvE by 7+ independent community sources), Rivers of Blood, and dual Scavenger's Curved Swords routinely outdamage higher-AR colossal weapons in boss fights through superior effective DPS, status effects, and weapon art burst. This ranking synthesizes peak AR at max upgrade, effective DPS (speed × damage ÷ stamina), and boss-effectiveness into a single composite score to identify the 100 weapons with the greatest damage potential across the base game and Shadow of the Erdtree DLC.

**Methodology and stat assumptions**: Peak AR values use **80 in primary scaling stat(s)** at max upgrade (+25 Smithing or +10 Somber). For infusable weapons, the best affinity for maximum AR is listed. "Quality" values reference 99 all stats from the Drake Ravenwolf Steam guide (2,700+ hand-recorded data points). Two-handing bonus (1.5× STR) is noted where it significantly changes the damage profile. Composite ranking weights Peak AR (35%), Boss Effectiveness (35%), and Effective DPS (30%). All AR values sourced from Fextralife wiki, tclark.io calculator, tarnished.dev, and community testing. Values marked with ~ are community-estimated within ±20 AR.

## What makes the top 10 truly dominant

The top 10 weapons separate themselves through excellence across multiple damage metrics simultaneously. **Giant-Crusher** dominates peak AR with ~965 pure physical damage two-handed — pure physical means no split-damage penalty through boss defenses, and Strike damage is universally effective against armored enemies. With Royal Knight's Resolve (+80% next attack), jump attacks reach **2,000–3,000+ damage** per hit. Power-stanced dual Giant-Crushers with jump attack buffs (Claw Talisman, Raptor's Black Feathers) can kill endgame bosses in 6–8 hits.

**Blasphemous Blade** earns the #2 overall spot despite moderate raw AR because Taker's Flames fundamentally changes PvE economics. The weapon art deals massive fire AoE while healing **10% max HP + 150 flat** per target hit, plus the passive heals 4% + 40 on any enemy death while held. This eliminates the need for healing flasks in most encounters, creating an insurmountable practical advantage. Seven independent community tier lists rank it #1 for PvE.

**Rivers of Blood** at #3 exemplifies how status effects transcend raw AR. Its ~634 AR is unimpressive, but Corpse Piler applies approximately **330 cumulative bleed buildup** across its multi-hit flurry at 80 ARC. Against bleed-susceptible bosses, this triggers hemorrhage (dealing ~15% max HP as burst damage) every 1–2 skill uses. The weapon became so dominant it was used by the legendary "Let Me Solo Her" player to farm thousands of Malenia victories.

## DLC weapons reshaped the meta significantly

Shadow of the Erdtree introduced eight new weapon categories and approximately 100 new weapons. Several immediately entered the top tier. The **Great Katana** (infusable) became arguably the most versatile DLC weapon — Occult affinity at 80 ARC produces **703 AR with 108 bleed buildup**, nearly matching Keen's 708 AR while providing double the status effect. With Lion's Claw as its Ash of War, it combines the reach of a greatsword with katana-speed recovery.

The **Backhand Blade** at only **579 AR** (Keen, 80 DEX) ranks #19 because its effective DPS far exceeds what its AR suggests. As a paired weapon weighing just 2.0 units, it attacks faster than any comparable weapon while the Blind Spot AoW provides i-frames during its dash-thrust. Blood or Cold infusion turns it into a status-effect machine. It was banned in competitive PvP tournaments despite nerfs in Patches 1.13 and 1.14.

**Bloodfiend's Arm** brought a new dimension to colossal weapons. Its 204 blood loss buildup is the highest in the entire game, and multi-hit charged R2s stack bleed at a rate previously impossible for heavy weapons. Combined with the Cragblade Ash of War for enhanced stance damage, it creates a feedback loop of stagger into bleed into stagger that trivializes many bosses. **Milady** established Light Greatswords as a top-tier weapon class, offering greatsword-level damage at near-straight-sword speed with the devastating Wing Stance AoW.

## Occult infusion is the hidden king of damage

One of the most important findings for a damage-focused companion tool: **Occult infusion at 80 ARC outperforms both Heavy (80 STR) and Keen (80 DEX) on approximately 9 out of 10 infusable weapons** according to extensive community testing. The Giant-Crusher gains +237 AR from Occult infusion alone, and on weapons with innate bleed, Occult preserves most bleed buildup while delivering significantly higher physical AR than Blood infusion.

The key Occult standouts include the **Great Omenkiller Cleaver** at 745 AR + 115 bleed (only ~30 AR less than the best Heavy greataxe), **Scavenger's Curved Sword** at 531 AR + 87 bleed for power-stancing, and virtually any innate-bleed weapon. The optimal bleed setup is often Occult on one weapon and Blood on its pair, maximizing both physical damage and status buildup simultaneously. Combined with White Mask (+10% attack on bleed proc) and Lord of Blood's Exultation (+20% on nearby bleed), this creates a self-reinforcing damage amplification loop.

## Effective DPS tells a different story than raw AR

The Drake Ravenwolf Steam guide introduced "DPSb" (damage per stamina bar) as a critical metric, and it dramatically reorders the hierarchy. **Venomous Fang** at just 572 AR achieves **11,440 DPSb** — nearly double the Giant-Crusher's 5,988 DPSb at 998 AR. The **Zamor Curved Sword** (779 AR) reaches 10,906 DPSb thanks to its unique fast moveset. **Bloodhound's Fang** (944 AR) achieves 8,496 DPSb, an exceptional combination of high per-hit damage and reasonable speed.

For boss encounters specifically, weapon arts matter as much as basic attacks. **Flame of the Redmanes** provides massive stance damage on any weapon it can be equipped to. **Lion's Claw** offers a hyper-armored forward flip slam with exceptional stagger and damage. **Spinning Strikes** on a Nightrider Glaive with buffs can reportedly achieve **20,000+ DPS over 5 seconds**. The Fire Knight's Greatsword with Flame Spear AoW can deal **6,900 damage per skill use** against fire-vulnerable enemies, far exceeding what any basic attack combo achieves.

Power-stancing (dual-wielding) adds approximately **70% more damage per attack cycle**, making it the highest raw DPS option in the game. Dual colossal weapons with jump attacks represent the absolute ceiling for per-hit damage, while dual curved swords offer the fastest sustained DPS for status-susceptible targets.

## Weapon types absent from the top 100 and why

Several weapon categories have **no representatives or minimal presence** in the top 100, reflecting fundamental design constraints:

- **Torches**: Excluded entirely. Torches are utility items with negligible damage output, designed for lighting and Beast-repelling rather than combat. Peak AR values are well below 300.

- **Light Bows**: No representatives. Maximum AR of ~305 (Misbegotten Shortbow) is far too low. Bows fundamentally lack the DPS to compete — they serve as supplementary tools for pulling enemies and applying status effects, not primary damage.

- **Crossbows** (except Jar Cannon/Rabbath's): Standard crossbows have no scaling, capping their damage potential. The Pulley Crossbow fires 3 bolts (~438 effective AR) but remains niche. Only ballistas achieve competitive per-shot damage.

- **Regular Daggers**: Extremely low AR (max ~490 at 99 all stats). The Misericorde has a 140 crit modifier making it exceptional for ripostes specifically, but its regular attack damage is bottom-tier. Daggers function as crit-fishing tools, not sustained damage dealers.

- **Most Perfume Bottles**: Very low raw AR (~350 range). They serve as elemental utility and status application rather than primary damage. Lightning Perfume Bottle earns a spot for its exceptional elemental coverage, but other variants fall short.

- **Most Throwing Blades**: Smithscript weapons cap around ~500–550 AR. The throwing mechanic is FP-free but deals reduced damage at range with falloff. Fun but fundamentally outclassed for damage purposes.

- **Generic Beast Claws**: The regular Beast Claw is considered one of the weakest DLC weapon types with high stamina drain and poor damage. Only Red Bear's Claw escapes this through innate bleed and its unique aggressive moveset.

## Stat archetypes and their best weapons at a glance

**Pure STR (66+ STR, 2H)**: Giant-Crusher, Prelate's Inferno Crozier, Ruins Greatsword, Greatsword, Brick Hammer. Two-handing at 54 STR achieves effective 81 STR, enabling massive damage with minimal stat spread.

**Pure DEX (80 DEX)**: Hand of Malenia, Nagakiba (Keen), Backhand Blade (Keen), Godskin Peeler (Keen), Great Katana (Keen). Fast, precise weapons with strong status synergy.

**Quality (40/40 or 54/54)**: Bloodhound's Fang, Golem's Halberd, Duelist Greataxe, Rakshasa's Great Katana. Benefits from the widest weapon selection but demands high stat investment.

**INT Hybrid (40+ INT)**: Moonveil, Dark Moon Greatsword, Wing of Astel, Carian Thrusting Shield, Death's Poker. Pairs melee with sorcery casting versatility.

**FTH Hybrid (40+ FTH)**: Blasphemous Blade, Sacred Relic Sword, Euporia, Coded Sword. Strong weapon arts that scale with Faith, plus incantation casting.

**ARC Hybrid (40+ ARC)**: Rivers of Blood, Scavenger's Curved Sword (Occult), Mohgwyn's Sacred Spear, Bloodfiend's Arm, Great Katana (Occult). Bleed-focused builds that scale status effects and physical damage simultaneously through Occult infusion.

**Stat-Efficient Picks** (high returns for low investment): Bloodhound's Fang (no infusion needed, early access), Claymore (works at any stat level), Nagakiba (good at 40 DEX, excellent at 80), Cross-Naginata (innate bleed, low requirements), Backhand Blade (2.0 weight, S DEX scaling).

## Conclusion

This ranking reveals three fundamental truths about Elden Ring's damage meta. First, **pure physical damage is king for boss fights** — the top 5 weapons by practical boss DPS are all either pure physical or overcome split-damage penalties through overwhelming status effects. Staff of the Avatar holds the raw AR crown at 1,089, but Giant-Crusher's ~965 pure-physical AR delivers more actual damage against almost every boss in the game.

Second, **effective DPS often inversely correlates with per-hit AR**. Fist weapons, curved swords, and backhand blades deliver 1.5–2× the damage per stamina bar compared to colossal weapons despite showing 40–50% lower AR on paper. The Backhand Blade at 579 AR may functionally out-damage an 800 AR greatsword in a 10-second boss window.

Third, **the Shadow of the Erdtree DLC materially improved weapon diversity at the top**. Great Katanas, Light Greatswords, and Backhand Blades all earned multiple top-25 placements, and the Bloodfiend's Arm introduced the concept of a bleed-focused colossal weapon that competes with pure-physical builds. The infusable Great Katana with Occult affinity may be the single most well-rounded weapon in the complete game — high AR, strong bleed, great reach, flexible Ash of War options, and available within the first hour of DLC content.

For the companion tool, the most critical data points to surface are: Peak AR with specified affinity and stat spread (use tclark.io calculator data), DPSb ratios from the Drake Ravenwolf dataset, and status buildup values that scale with ARC investment. These three numbers together predict real-world damage output far better than any single metric alone.
